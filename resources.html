No Culture Shock Here!

Taniti is a welcoming destination where international travelers feel at home. While our indigenous population is proud of its local culture, English is widely spoken throughout Taniti City and in tourist areas like Yellow Leaf Bay and Merriton Landing. The local staff are multilingual and eager to assist, and our hospital features many English-speaking employees for your peace of mind. Also feel free to spend your US dollars as they are accepted widely on the island.

Local Laws & Safety

To ensure a safe and enjoyable visit, please note the following important regulations:

  • Alcohol is not served or sold between the hours of midnight and 9:00 a.m. The legal drinking age is 18, though enforcement varies.
  • If you choose to rent bicycles or helmets, helmets are required by law.
  • While violent crime is very rare, be vigilant against pickpocketing in crowded areas.
  • Taniti observes many national holidays; during these times, many attractions and restaurants will be closed, so plan accordingly.

Travel Arrangements

Traveling to Taniti is convenient for visitors from the United States and Europe.

  • We use the U.S. Dollar (USD). However, many businesses also accept Euros and Yen, and banks facilitate currency exchange.
  • Our power outlets are 120 volts, which is the same standard as in the United States—no adapters required.
  • Public buses run from 5:00 a.m. to 11:00 p.m. daily. For more flexibility, rental cars and taxis are readily available.
